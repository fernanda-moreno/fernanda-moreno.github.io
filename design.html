Skills used: Adobe XD, Paper Prototyping, User Experience Research & Design

NASA HUNCH Calendar Section

Skills used: WordPress, HTML, CSS, Adobe XD

As a software development and design intern at NASA Ames Research Center, I was given the task to design a calendar section for the NASA HUNCH website in order to increase student awareness of program events throughout the nation. The calendar was implemented through a WordPress plug-in and customized with CSS so that the font and colors matched the rest of the website. As events are added to the NASA HUNCH Google Calendar, the calendar and upcoming events section will update automatically. The image below captures how the calendar appears on the existing Get Involved page. Check it out on the live site here.

Alcohol Education VR Prototype

Skills used: User Research, Product Design, Video making & editing

For my Educational Technology class, I worked with a team to design an interactive film prototype for an alcohol education virtual reality simulation. In our product, users are taken through a personalized experience of alcohol usage on a college campus in an effort to raise awareness of the dangers of alcohol misuse and tackle misconceptions. We created a personalization quiz that determines users' preferred social setting and the effects that alochol would have on them throughout the simulation. Throughout the experience, users would make decisions that change the course of the simulation. The prototype below consists of a series of videos that follow the experience of a pseudo college freshman named Alex.

Organizational Branding Project

Skills used: Adobe - Illustrator, Dimension

For this project, I designed a logo and brand identity system for a coffee shop dedicated to people who are involved in or aspire to work in the field of technology. I named the brand Code & Coffee Co. and envisioned it as a productive space for students in tech as well as professional software engineers and developers. The simplistic and iconic logo for Code & Coffee Co. illustrates a cup filled with hot coffee marked by the HTML break symbol to demonstrate a coffee break. The image represents the main product sold at the company and the HTML tag signifies that coding and technology are incorporated into the theme of this coffee shop.

Travel Troopers App & Map Prototype

Skills used: Adobe XD, physical model, Wizard of Oz Prototyping

While studying abroad in Georgia Tech's Barcelona Summer Program (2018), I took a class on User Interface Design. For our group project throughout the entire course, my team and I created a prototype of an app and augmented reality map interface that helped groups traveling abroad find the best local restaurants as recommended by their friends. In addition to recommendations, our app and map interfaces included the ability to provide directions to these locations and facilitate communication among groups of friends.

Futuristic Pet Bowl

Skills used: Sketching, paper interface

In my UI Design class, I was assigned an open-ended individual project where I had to design, layout, and mockup a Family Messaging System that a busy family could use to communicate with each other across time and space. My idea was to create something that would allow a family to communicate in terms of when their pet was last fed or if it had already been fed by another family member. The invention I designed was a futuristic pet bowl that recorded the time that food was last placed into the pet's bowl so members of the family would know if their pet needed their food to be served.
